These are the first of the Sherlock Holmes short stories, originally published as single stories in the Strand Magazine from July 1891 to June 1892. The book was published in England on 14 October 1892 by George Newnes Ltd and in a US Edition on 15 October by Harper. The initial combined print run was 14,500 copies.

The first six stories in this collection are:

  1. A Scandal in Bohemia; Client: The King of Bohemia
    • This was the first of Arthur Conan Doyle's 56 Sherlock Holmes short stories to be published in Strand Magazine and the first Sherlock Holmes story illustrated by Sidney Paget.
    • Two of the four Sherlock Holmes novels – A Study in Scarlet and The Sign of the Four – preceded the short story cycle.
    • Doyle ranked A Scandal in Bohemia fifth in his list of his twelve favourite Holmes stories.
  2. The Adventure of the Red-Headed League; Client: Jabez Wilson
  3. A Case of Identity; Client: Mary Sutherland
  4. The Boscombe Valley Mystery; Client: Alice Turner
  5. The Five Orange Pips; Client: John Openshaw
  6. The Man with the Twisted Lip; Client: Mrs. St. Clair

The other six stories in this collection are:

  • The Adventure of the Blue Carbuncle; Client: None
  • The Adventure of the Speckled Band; Client: Miss Helen Stoner
    1. It is one of four Sherlock Holmes stories that can be classified as a locked room mystery.
    2. The story was first published in Strand Magazine in February 1892, with illustrations by Sidney Paget.
    3. It was published under the different title “The Spotted Band” in New York World in August 1905.
  • The Adventure of the Engineer’s Thumb; Client: Victor Hatherley
  • The Adventure of the Noble Bachelor; Client: Lord Robert St. Simon
  • The Adventure of the Beryl Coronet; Client: Alexander Holder
  • The Adventure of the Copper Beeches; Client: Violet Hunter
